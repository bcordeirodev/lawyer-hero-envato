üìß EmailJS Configuration

The template uses EmailJS for contact form functionality. Follow these steps to set it up:

Step 1: Create EmailJS Account

  1. Visit EmailJS.com
  2. Sign up for a free account
  3. Verify your email address

Step 2: Setup Email Service

  1. Go to Email Services in your dashboard
  2. Click Add New Service
  3. Choose your email provider (Gmail recommended)
  4. Follow the authentication steps

Step 3: Create Email Template

  1. Go to Email Templates
  2. Click Create New Template
  3. Use the following template structure:
Subject: New Contact from {{from_name}} Hello, You have received a new contact form submission: Name: {{from_name}} Email: {{from_email}} Phone: {{phone}} Subject: {{subject}} Message: {{message}} --- Sent from your lawyer website contact form.

Step 4: Get Your Credentials

  1. Note your Service ID from Email Services
  2. Note your Template ID from Email Templates
  3. Get your Public Key from Account settings

Step 5: Configure Environment Variables

Create a .env.local file in your project root:

NEXT_PUBLIC_EMAILJS_SERVICE_ID=your_service_id NEXT_PUBLIC_EMAILJS_TEMPLATE_ID=your_template_id NEXT_PUBLIC_EMAILJS_PUBLIC_KEY=your_public_key
‚úÖ Done! Your contact form will now send emails to your configured address.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Compliance & Ethics

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Notice: This template has been designed with legal advertising ethics in mind. However, you are responsible for ensuring your content complies with your local bar association's rules.

üö´ What to Avoid

  • Guaranteed Results: Never promise specific outcomes or success rates
  • Unproven Specializations: Only claim specializations officially recognized by your bar
  • Direct Solicitation: Avoid aggressive calls-to-action like "Hire Now" or "Free Consultation"
  • Misleading Statistics: Don't display case numbers or success percentages without context
  • Superlatives: Avoid terms like "best," "top," or "leading" without substantiation

‚úÖ Best Practices

  • Use Neutral Language: "Experienced in..." instead of "Expert in..."
  • Informational Content: Focus on education rather than persuasion
  • Clear Disclaimers: Include notices about case uniqueness and no guarantee of results
  • Proper Credentials: Only display verified bar admissions and certifications
  • Professional Tone: Maintain dignity and avoid sensationalism

üìù Template Compliance Features

This template includes several compliance-friendly features:

  • Neutral language in all default content
  • Legal disclaimer in footer
  • "Contact" instead of "Hire" call-to-action buttons
  • Informational rather than promotional tone
  • No specific performance metrics or guarantees

üåç Jurisdiction-Specific Rules

Important: Legal advertising rules vary by jurisdiction. Common regulations include:
  • USA: ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct
  • UK: Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) guidelines
  • Canada: Provincial Law Society regulations
  • Australia: Legal Services Commission rules
  • Brazil: OAB Provimento 205/2021

üîç Before Going Live Checklist

  1. Review all content for compliance with local rules
  2. Verify all credentials and certifications are accurate
  3. Ensure disclaimers are visible and clear
  4. Remove any promotional language or guarantees
  5. Consider having your bar association review the site
‚úÖ Compliance First: When in doubt, err on the side of caution. Professional responsibility should always take precedence over marketing appeal.
